# Discord Server Channel Definitions

This document outlines the purpose and intended use for every channel in our server to ensure an organized, secure, and productive environment.

---

## 📢 1. Welcome & Information
*These channels are the front door to our community. They handle onboarding, rules, and global announcements.*

*   **`#welcome`**
    *   **Purpose**: The landing page for all new members. Features our UI Logo Banner and triggers the "Call To Arms" requirement where members read our manifesto and select their Skill Level Rating (1-5).
*   **`#rules`**
    *   **Purpose**: The immutable law of the server. Outlines our Code Sharing Policy, Zero Tolerance Malware protocol, Ban Appeals process, and Community Conduct. (Rule screening must be accepted before participating).
*   **`#announcements`**
    *   **Purpose**: Read-only channel. A space for Admins and Community Leaders to post important updates, new events, structural changes, or community milestones.
*   **`#roles`**
    *   **Purpose**: Where members can come to self-assign secondary tags (e.g., "Python Learner," "Mentor," web frameworks) using reaction roles.
*   **`#github-shared-links`**
    *   **Purpose**: Automated feed channel hooked to our GitHub Webhooks, streaming activity, new repos, and commits from the community archive.

---

## 🤝 2. Community & Collaboration
*The social hub of the server, designed for networking, showing off work, and sharing resources.*

*   **`#resources`**
    *   **Purpose**: A curated collection of tutorials, guides, verified external sites, and learning materials.
*   **`#showcase`**
    *   **Purpose**: A dedicated place for members to share their finished projects, receive feedback, and collaborate with peers on open-source initiatives.
*   **`#memes`**
    *   **Purpose**: A lighthearted, safe space for tech and programming-related humor. Keep it clean and within the bounds of "The Human Element" rules.
*   **`#career-growth`**
    *   **Purpose**: An active feed for sharing job opportunities, internship alerts, interview preparation tips, and resume reviews.

---

## 🛠️ 3. Support & Technical Help
*The core engine of learning. Where questions are asked, code is reviewed, and problems are solved.*

*   **`#help-desk`**
    *   **Purpose**: A primary area for developers to ask for technical assistance utilizing a dedicated ticketing system bot (like Helper.gg). Keeps complex issues organized.
*   **`#code-reviews`**
    *   **Purpose**: A public space where members can post code snippets for pro-level feedback or debugging. OpenCode AI and Gemini Flash are active here to assist. (Must adhere to public sharing rules).
*   **`#quick-questions`**
    *   **Purpose**: For short, simple questions that don't require deep discussion or dedicated tickets, keeping other help channels uncluttered.
*   **`#learning-groups`**
    *   **Purpose**: Sub-channels for specific study cohorts, such as zero-to-employed bootcamps or groups focusing on fundamental algorithms.

---

## 💻 4. Language-Specific Categories
*Focused technical discussion spaces grouped by technology to prevent cross-talk and clutter.*

*   **Web Development**:
    *   `#html-css`, `#javascript`, `#react`, `#angular`
*   **Backend & Data**:
    *   `#python`, `#java`, `#c-plus-plus`, `#rust`
*   **Specialty Topics**:
    *   `#ai-machine-learning`, `#devops`, `#cybersecurity`

---

## 🔒 5. The Review Chamber (Private Channels)
*High-security zones with elevated controls.*

*   **`#Private-Review-[ID]` (Dynamic)**
    *   **Purpose**: Initiated upon request. Used for proprietary work, sensitive scripts, reviewing potential malware flags (false positives), or deep-dives into code that cannot immediately be public.
    *   **Controls**: Managed strictly by Admins, Moderators, and Bots.
    *   **The Blackout Protocol**: To maintain absolute security, a bot workflow is configured to automatically purge all messages, code, and files from these channels the moment all participating members exit the channel, ensuring a Total Purge.
